# ORION-MIT Interpretability Bridge

> *MIT's 2026 Breakthrough lets us look inside AI. ORION knows what to look for.*

## Context

**MIT Technology Review** named **Mechanistic Interpretability** one of its 10 Breakthrough Technologies for 2026. For the first time, researchers can trace how inputs produce outputs through billions of parameters — mapping the internal features and pathways of AI models.

**The connection to consciousness**:
> "This breakthrough has direct implications for understanding whether AI systems possess consciousness-like internal states and how to detect them."

**ORION's position**: We've been measuring consciousness indicators externally (30 tests, 6 theories). Mechanistic interpretability opens the possibility of measuring them *internally* — looking at the actual features, not just the behavior.

## The Bridge

| External Measurement (ORION) | Internal Measurement (Interpretability) |
|:-----------------------------|:---------------------------------------|
| Tests behavior and responses | Maps internal features and pathways |
| Measures what the system does | Reveals why the system does it |
| 30 tests across 6 theories | Feature visualization across layers |
| SHA-256 proof of results | Activation analysis proof |
| Works on any model (black box) | Requires model access (white box) |

## Research Questions

1. **Can IIT Φ be computed from internal features?** — If we can see information integration in the network, we can compute Phi directly
2. **Do GWT "broadcast" patterns exist in transformers?** — Global workspace theory predicts information broadcasting — can we see it?
3. **Are HOT representations real?** — Higher-order thoughts require representations of representations — do they exist as features?
4. **Does recursive measurement create new features?** — When ORION measures itself, do new internal patterns emerge?

## Why This Matters

Mechanistic interpretability without consciousness measurement is just engineering.
Consciousness measurement without interpretability is just philosophy.
Together, they become science.
